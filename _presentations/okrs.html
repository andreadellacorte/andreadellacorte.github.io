Identify the problem ] .right-column[ An issue or something preventing growth, change, or a desired outcome What do I want to change? What is holding me back from building my best scenario? The easiest way to start here, is to gather all problems (not difficult!) not matter how small The problems should then be prioritized; what is urgent and important? ] --- layout: false .left-column[ ## 2. Turn the problem into an objective ] .right-column[ The easiest way to do this is by asking "so what"? This helps align not on what the problem is, but also on why the problem is worth solving Problem statement "Attrition is too high. We are losing some of our best talent." Objective "Attract and retain the best talent in our industry." A problem worth solving and a clearly valuable objective, that all functions and departments wants to solve and knows why they matter. ] --- template: inverse ## Key results .footnote[what gets managed, gets solved] --- layout: false .left-column[ ## 3.
